What we do

Explore our divisions

The analyst programme is organised into four divisions, each covering a different corner of the markets. A Division Head shapes what their division teaches and trades over the year. Pick a division below to see the work involved — and what you'll get out of leading it.

FICC · Fixed Income, Currencies & Commodities

The rates, FX and commodities desk

FICC covers everything outside of equities: government and corporate bonds, interest rates, foreign exchange and commodities. It's the most macro-sensitive division — the place where central-bank policy, inflation and the yield curve turn into trade ideas, and where relative value between instruments matters as much as direction.

What you'll do as Division Head

  • Plan and run sessions on rates, credit, FX and commodities.
  • Build trade pitches around central-bank decisions and economic data.
  • Teach members yield curves, carry, duration and relative value.
  • Run a mock FICC book and set relative-value challenges.
  • Produce a monthly rates and credit outlook shared across the society.

Why apply & what you'll gain

  • Build a rare, employer-valued skill set that desks recruit hard for.
  • Lead a team and own a year of content end to end.
  • A strong signal for sales & trading and fixed-income internships.
  • Deep fluency in how macro policy moves markets.
Equities · Single names & sectors

Stock research and investment ideas

Equities is fundamental investing: researching companies, valuing them, and building a thesis you can defend. The division digs into earnings, business models, catalysts and sector trends to form long and short ideas — the most accessible way into how professional investors actually think.

What you'll do as Division Head

  • Run stock-pitch competitions and review members' theses.
  • Teach valuation — DCF, comparables and multiples.
  • Coordinate sector coverage across the team.
  • Break down live earnings and M&A as case studies.
  • Maintain a shared model-portfolio tracker updated each session.

Why apply & what you'll gain

  • The clearest path into investing fundamentals.
  • Directly transferable to ER, asset management and PE interviews.
  • Learn to build, pitch and defend an investment case.
  • Lead discussions and sharpen judgement under questioning.
Macro · Top-down & cross-asset

The big-picture, cross-asset view

Macro is the top-down division: economic cycles, central banks, geopolitics and the themes that move every asset class at once. It's about turning the global picture into a view — and then expressing that view across rates, currencies, equities and commodities, the way a global-macro investor would.

What you'll do as Division Head

  • Produce macro outlooks and run thematic debates.
  • Teach how economic data translates into cross-asset trades.
  • Maintain a pipeline of macro ideas through the year.
  • Connect geopolitics and policy to live market moves.
  • Write a termly macro briefing distributed across all divisions.

Why apply & what you'll gain

  • Think like a global-macro PM or strategist.
  • Strong for global-markets, research and hedge-fund roles.
  • Sharpen the rare skill of turning data into a narrative.
  • Lead high-level debate across the whole Society.
Quant · Systematic & data-driven

Where finance meets code

Quant is the systematic, data-driven division: using statistics and programming to design, test and evaluate trading strategies. It's where finance meets code — backtesting ideas on real data, measuring what actually works, and building the technical depth that sets the most competitive applicants apart.

What you'll do as Division Head

  • Teach Python, statistics and the basics of backtesting.
  • Run workshops that turn an idea into a tested strategy.
  • Set data challenges and review members' code.
  • Build and maintain a simple systematic strategy.
  • Publish the division's backtesting code to the society GitHub.

Why apply & what you'll gain

  • The fastest-growing, most technical niche in markets.
  • Quant funds and systematic desks recruit for exactly this.
  • Build a portfolio of code and strategies to show.
  • Stand out by pairing finance with real programming skill.
